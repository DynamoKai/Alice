The Code Book

written by Simon Singh

This is a rewrite project focusing on interactive structure


Practically since humans began writing, they have been writing in code, and ciphers have decided the fates pf empires throughout recorded history. In The Code Book, Simon Singh offers a sweeping history of the subject of encryption. Combining a storyteller's sense of drama with a scientist's appreciation for technical perfection, he traces the evolution of its methods and reveals its dramatic effects on wars, nations, and individual lives.


From Mary, Queen of Scots, trapped by her own code and put to death by Queen Elizabeth, to the Navajo Code talkers who helped the Allies win World War II, to the incredible (and incredibly simple) breakthrough that made internet commerce secure, The Code Book tell the story of the most powerful intellectual weapon ever known: secrecy.


Woven throughout are clear technological and mathematical explanations, and portrayals of the remarkable personalities---many courageous, some villainous, and all obsessive---who wrote and broke the worlds's most diffcult codes. Accessible, compelling, and remarkably far-reaching, this book will forever alter your view of history, what drives it, and how private that e-mail you just sent really is.
